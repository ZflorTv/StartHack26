# Battle System

> *One headline. One portfolio. Three scores. Battle Mode distills the entire investing education into a 60-second duel with the market.*

---

## 🌟 The Fantasy

You're a portfolio manager who just got handed a breaking news headline. You have seconds to build a portfolio that will survive what's coming. It's not about predicting the future — it's about proving you understand the present.

The player feels like a **financial first responder** — someone who can look at chaos and see opportunity, building the right garden for the storm that's already on the horizon.

## 🔄 The Loop

Battle Mode is a quick-challenge format designed for replayability:

1. **A real-world headline appears** — "Fed raises rates 75 basis points" or "Oil prices surge on Middle East tensions"
2. **Choose your risk profile** — Zen, Meadow, or Jungle. This isn't just flavor — it sets the benchmark your portfolio will be judged against.
3. **Build your portfolio using sliders** — Each asset class from 0% to 50%, total allocation between 50% and 100%. Every slider is a commitment.
4. **Submit and watch** — The market event plays out. Your plants react. Your scores calculate in real time.
5. **Three-axis results** — Not one score, but three. You're judged on consistency, performance, AND responsibility.

The entire round takes under 90 seconds. But the strategic depth is enormous.

## 🎲 The Tension & Drama

The drama in Battle Mode comes from the **three-axis scoring system** — because optimizing for one axis often means sacrificing another:

### Axis 1: Risk Reliability (30% of final score)
*"Did you actually build what you said you'd build?"*

If you declared Jungle (aggressive) but loaded up on bonds, your Risk Reliability score tanks. The game is teaching **self-awareness** — know your risk tolerance, then actually follow it. Investors who say they're aggressive but flee at the first downturn are the most common real-world failure mode.

### Axis 2: Profitability (40% of final score)
*"Did your garden survive the weather?"*

Pure performance. How did your portfolio do when the event hit? An oil crisis rewards commodity-heavy portfolios. A rate hike punishes bond-heavy ones. This axis rewards **financial knowledge** — understanding which assets respond to which events.

### Axis 3: ESG Score (30% of final score)
*"Did you cut corners to win?"*

This is the moral dimension. Loading up on Coal Bush (fossil fuels) or Leveraged Vine (margin trading) gives you short-term performance — but devastates your ESG score. Coal Bush alone costs 80 points per allocation percentage. The game teaches that **sustainability isn't just ethics — it's risk management**.

### The Impossible Choice

The brilliance of three-axis scoring is that there's no "perfect" portfolio. A Jungle player who loads up on crypto might ace Profitability during a tech bubble — but their ESG score suffers and their Risk Reliability only works if they declared aggressive. A Zen player who plays it safe scores high on Reliability but may underperform during boom events.

**Every battle is a lesson in trade-offs** — the most important concept in investing, taught through a 60-second game.

## 🧠 The Psychology

Battle Mode taps into several powerful motivators:

- **Competence Testing** — "I think I understand how markets work. Let me prove it." Each headline is a pop quiz that feels like a game.
- **Speed and Decisiveness** — The time pressure forces intuitive decisions, which reveals what the player has actually internalized versus what they merely memorized.
- **Multi-dimensional Thinking** — Three scores force players to think holistically. You can't min-max your way to victory. This mirrors real investing, where returns, risk, and responsibility must all be balanced.
- **Surprise and Variety** — Random headlines mean no two battles are the same. The player can't memorize answers — they must understand principles.

## 💰 The Business Connection

Battle Mode is the **viral engine** of Growden:

- **Event Activation Ready:** At conferences, school workshops, or PostFinance events, Battle Mode is a perfect 5-minute demo. Hand someone a phone, show them a headline, watch them build a portfolio. Instant engagement.
- **Shareable Scores:** Three-axis results create interesting score profiles. "I got 90 on Profitability but only 40 on ESG" is a conversation starter.
- **Competitive Hooks:** When multiplayer launches, Battle Mode becomes a real-time portfolio duel. Same headline, two players, who builds the better garden? This is the kind of mechanic that drives app store features and social media moments.
- **Session Extension:** Campaign mode has a natural end (level 10). Battle Mode is infinitely replayable. It's the mechanic that turns a 15-minute educational game into a daily habit.

## ⚠️ Design Gaps & Open Questions

- ⚠️ **Design Gap** — *Battle Mode is currently single-player only. The design calls for Firebase-powered real-time multiplayer (same headline, head-to-head scoring), but this isn't implemented. The social competitive layer is the difference between a feature and a viral mechanic.*
- ⚠️ **Design Gap** — *How many unique headlines exist? If the pool is small, experienced players will memorize optimal portfolios per headline, undermining the learning objective. A headline generation system (possibly AI-powered) would solve this.*
- ⚠️ **Design Gap** — *The ESG axis at 30% weight is a strong design statement. Is it calibrated correctly? Too heavy and it feels preachy. Too light and it's ignorable. Player testing needed.*

## 🔮 Design Opportunities

1. **Live Headline Mode:** Connect to real financial news APIs. Today's actual headlines become today's battle. Players learn by responding to real events in real time — then see how markets actually moved the next day. This collapses the gap between game and reality.

2. **Tournament Format:** Weekly tournaments where all players face the same 5 headlines. Global leaderboard. Top performers earn exclusive plant skins or titles. This creates appointment gaming and community events.

3. **Team Battles:** Two players build one portfolio together, each controlling different asset classes. This teaches collaboration and specialization — and doubles the social surface area.
