# Progression & Mastery

> *From Seedling to Master Gardener — a journey where "getting better" means building the instincts of a real investor.*

---

## 🌟 The Fantasy

The player starts as a novice gardener with five plants and a simple sky. By the end, they're a weathered veteran who reads the clouds, knows every plant's temperament, and builds gardens that survive anything. The fantasy is **earned expertise** — the feeling of looking at a complex market event and thinking "I know exactly what to do."

## 🔄 The Loop

### The Ten-Level Campaign

Growden's campaign compresses ten years of market experience into ten levels, each lasting roughly 90 seconds:

| Level | What Unlocks | What the Player Learns |
|-------|-------------|----------------------|
| **1** | Starter kit (5 plants) | "My garden IS my portfolio" |
| **2–3** | More equities, bonds | Diversification saves you |
| **4–5** | Commodities, inflation events | Real assets hedge inflation |
| **6** | Junk Weed (first bad plant) | Not all bonds are safe |
| **7** | Coal Bush, Market Panic event | ESG risk is financial risk; don't panic sell |
| **8** | Leveraged Vine, Geopolitical Shock | Leverage destroys; defense protects |
| **9–10** | Full collection, Sector Mania | FOMO kills; patience wins |

**Critical design choice:** There is no failure state. Players always advance to the next level regardless of performance. But their score — and their garden's visible health — tells the truth. A player who panic-sold through five levels arrives at level 10 with fewer flowers, fewer plants, and a garden that looks battered. They advance, but they know they struggled.

This mirrors real investing: you don't "lose" at the stock market by being eliminated. You lose by making worse decisions than you could have.

### Score Tiers: The Mountain to Climb

| Tier | Score | What It Means |
|------|-------|---------------|
| 🌱 Seedling | < 50 | The player panicked. A lot. |
| 🌿 Sprout | 50–149 | Some good instincts, some costly mistakes |
| 🌳 Gardener | 150–299 | Solid understanding of diversification |
| 🌺 Botanist | 300–499 | Strong performance, few panic sells |
| 🏆 Master Gardener | 500+ | Elite. Held through storms, diversified perfectly |

Each tier is a badge of financial literacy. A Master Gardener has demonstrated — through actions, not answers — that they understand risk management, diversification, patience, and long-term thinking.

### Achievements: Ten Badges of Mastery

Achievements mark specific skills and behaviors:

- **Diamond Hands** — Held through 3+ market crashes without selling
- **Zen Master** — Completed the entire game with zero panic sells
- **ESG Champion** — Never held Coal Bush in the portfolio
- **Diversification Pro** — Maintained 5+ different plants throughout the game
- **Plant Collector** — Unlocked all 25 plants
- **High Roller** — Reached 500+ score

Each achievement triggers a confetti celebration — a micro-moment of delight that validates the player's growth.

## 🎲 The Tension & Drama

Progression creates tension through **visible consequences**:

- A player who panic-sold in level 3 has 25 fewer points AND fewer flowers to buy plants with. When level 6 unlocks Junk Weed, they might not have enough flowers to avoid it. Past mistakes echo forward.
- The diversification bonus (+20 points per level) rewards breadth. But buying more plants costs flowers. A player with a tight flower budget must choose: fewer, stronger plants? Or more plants at thinner allocations?
- Score tiers are public on the leaderboard. Everyone can see whether you're a Seedling or a Master Gardener. Status is on the line.

## 🧠 The Psychology

**Mastery Gradient:** The game uses a well-calibrated difficulty curve. Early levels feel achievable (60% light events). Later levels feel challenging (60% severe events). This creates a flow state where the player is always slightly stretched but never overwhelmed.

**Competence Signaling:** Tier names (Seedling → Master Gardener) give players a vocabulary to describe their skill level. "I'm a Botanist" means something. It's an identity, not just a number.

**Hidden Curriculum:** The most profound progression isn't visible in the score. It's in the player's changing behavior. A first-time player panic-sells during every storm. A second-time player hesitates. A third-time player holds confidently. The game is literally rewiring financial instincts — and the player can feel themselves getting better.

**Three Paths Up:** The three risk profiles (Zen, Meadow, Jungle) mean the same 10 levels play differently three times. A Zen player's perfect game looks nothing like a Jungle player's perfect game. This multiplies mastery surface area without multiplying content.

## 💰 The Business Connection

**Retention Architecture:** The progression system provides at minimum 3 full playthroughs (one per risk profile) with achievement hunting creating reasons for 5+ additional runs. Each playthrough takes 15 minutes — meaning the game provides 1–2 hours of deeply engaged content from a single campaign structure.

**Measurable Learning Outcomes:** Score tiers and achievements create trackable proxies for financial literacy. PostFinance can report: "78% of players who completed all 10 levels achieved Gardener tier or above" — a meaningful metric for educational impact assessment.

**Graduation Moment:** When a player reaches Master Gardener, they've demonstrated real investment principles. This is the natural moment for a call-to-action: "You've mastered the garden. Ready to grow a real portfolio? Open an account with PostFinance."

## ⚠️ Design Gaps & Open Questions

- ⚠️ **Design Gap** — *No prestige system after Master Gardener. What keeps a 500+ score player coming back? Seasonal rankings, prestige resets, or challenge modifiers could extend the endgame.*
- ⚠️ **Design Gap** — *The scoring system rewards holding above all else. Is there a scenario where selling IS the right call? If not, the game teaches a one-dimensional "always hold" lesson that may not serve sophisticated players.*
- ⚠️ **Design Gap** — *Achievement difficulty varies wildly. "Plant Collector" requires 10 full levels. "Zen Master" requires perfect discipline. Are these balanced in effort-to-satisfaction ratio?*

## 🔮 Design Opportunities

1. **Mentor System:** Master Gardener players can mentor Seedlings — reviewing their portfolios and suggesting improvements. This creates a social layer, extends engagement, and turns expertise into status.

2. **Mastery Challenges:** Post-campaign challenges that test specific skills in isolation. "Build a garden that survives three consecutive severe events." "Reach level 10 using only three plant types." Each challenge deepens a specific financial concept.

3. **Learning Report Card:** At the end of each playthrough, generate a personalized "Financial Skills Report" showing strengths (diversification, patience) and weaknesses (panic selling, overtrading). Shareable, printable, and directly tied to PostFinance's educational mission.
