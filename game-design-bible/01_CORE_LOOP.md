# Core Loop

> *Plant, weather, decide, learn — the four-beat rhythm that makes investing feel like gardening and gardening feel like strategy.*

---

## 🌟 The Fantasy

The player is a gardener facing an unpredictable sky. Every season brings a new weather pattern — some nurturing, some destructive. The thrill isn't in predicting the weather. It's in having built a garden resilient enough to handle anything.

Moment to moment, the player feels like a **calm strategist watching chaos unfold** — and being rewarded for preparation rather than reaction.

## 🔄 The Loop

### First 5 Minutes: "Oh, This Is About Money"

1. **Choose your gardening style** — Zen Garden (conservative), Meadow (balanced), or Jungle (aggressive). This is secretly a risk profile, but it feels like personality.
2. **Plant your first garden** — Pick from a starter kit of five plants (one equity tree, one bond bush, one cash grass, one commodity cactus, one crypto orchid). Each costs flowers — the in-game currency.
3. **First weather event hits** — Earnings Season. The sun shines, the trees grow. The player sees their apple tree pulse with green energy. Score goes up. They smile.
4. **Second event** — A calm day. Nothing happens. Plants grow slowly. The game whispers: *"Most investing is exactly this: patient, boring, steady."* If the player overtrades during the calm, they lose points.
5. **The "aha" moment** — They realize the game is teaching them something real. This isn't just a garden sim. The weather IS the market.

### First Session (15 Minutes): "I'm Getting Better at This"

Across 10 levels, the player faces escalating challenges:

- **Levels 1–3:** Gentle introduction. Earnings seasons, calm days, a first unemployment spike. The game teaches diversification by showing what happens when you don't have it.
- **Levels 4–6:** Complexity increases. Fed rate decisions, inflation shocks, tariff crises. New plants unlock — including the first **bad plant** (Junk Weed) that looks attractive but carries hidden risk.
- **Levels 7–9:** The real test. Market panics with double penalties for panic selling. Geopolitical shocks. The two-phase Sector Mania event where crypto moons 80% — then crashes 75%.
- **Level 10:** The final stress test. Everything at once. Severe amplitudes. The garden either stands or crumbles.

The core action cycle at every level:

```
🌱 Plant/Rebalance → ⛈️ Weather Event → 🤔 Decide (Hold/Rebalance/Sell) → 📊 See Results → 🌸 Earn Flowers → 🔄 Next Level
```

### Day 7 and Beyond: "I Want to Beat My Score"

After completing the campaign, the player knows their final rating — Seedling, Sprout, Gardener, Botanist, or Master Gardener. The question becomes: *can I do better?*

- **Replay with a different risk profile** — The Jungle path is wildly different from the Zen path
- **Battle Mode** — Quick-challenge rounds against random headlines, scored on three axes
- **Achievement hunting** — Diamond Hands (hold through 3 crashes), Zen Master (zero panic sells), Plant Collector (unlock all 25 plants)
- **Leaderboard climbing** — Top 10 scores create social competition

## 🎲 The Tension & Drama

The drama lives in the **decision moment**. When a tornado event hits and the player's portfolio is down 20%, they face three choices:

- **Hold Steady** (+15 points) — Trust the garden. The storm will pass.
- **Rebalance** (+5 points) — Adjust allocations. Thoughtful, but costly in a storm.
- **Panic Sell** (-25 points, or -50 during a Market Panic) — The worst choice, and the most tempting.

The game is designed so that **the emotionally correct choice (sell everything!) is almost always the strategically wrong choice**. This mirrors real investing perfectly — and it's the core lesson.

## 🧠 The Psychology

The loop taps into three powerful psychological drivers:

1. **Mastery** — Every replay teaches something new. The player starts recognizing event patterns, anticipating which plants will suffer, and pre-positioning their garden. Getting better feels tangible.

2. **Loss Aversion** — Watching plants wilt hurts more than watching them bloom feels good. This emotional asymmetry teaches the real pain of panic selling — not through a lecture, but through a felt experience.

3. **Completion Drive** — 10 levels, 25 plants to unlock, 10 achievements to earn, 5 score tiers to reach. The game gives the player a clear mountain to climb, with multiple paths up.

## 💰 The Business Connection

The core loop is designed for **15-minute sessions** — perfect for mobile engagement patterns. Short enough to play on a commute, deep enough to come back tomorrow.

**Retention drivers:** Achievement hunting, leaderboard competition, and three distinct risk profiles create at minimum 3x replay value from a single content investment.

**Conversion path:** Every session reinforces financial concepts using PostFinance's brand language. When the player eventually opens a real brokerage account, they already think in terms of diversification, risk profiles, and long-term holding — PostFinance's core product philosophy.

## ⚠️ Design Gaps & Open Questions

- ⚠️ **Design Gap** — *What happens after the player has completed all 10 levels on all 3 risk profiles? The content well has a floor. Seasonal events or procedurally generated scenarios would extend the loop.*
- ⚠️ **Design Gap** — *The "Rebalance" decision during events is mechanically thin — it's a single button press. Could this become a mini-game where the player actually adjusts sliders under time pressure?*

## 🔮 Design Opportunities

1. **Daily Weather Challenge:** A single daily event that all players face simultaneously — creating a shared community moment and a reason to open the app every day. "Today's weather: Fed Rate Hike. How did your garden do?"

2. **Streak Rewards:** Consecutive daily play earns bonus flowers and exclusive plant variants. This builds habit without requiring long sessions.

3. **Difficulty Modifiers:** After completing the campaign, unlock "Climate Change Mode" (more severe events), "Speed Run" (faster event cadence), or "Drought" (start with fewer flowers). Each creates a meaningfully different challenge from the same content.
